From War Refugee to Environmental Champion

The Inspiring Journey of James Mulbah

James Mulbah's amazing story shows how he went from being a war refugee to becoming a leader in protecting nature. His journey teaches us about strength, learning, and how to care for our planet in ways that work today while respecting old traditions.

Early Life in Liberia

1990s - Monrovia, Liberia

When James was young, his country Liberia was at war. As the oldest child, he walked for two weeks with his parents to escape to Guinea. In their new home, James learned to be strong by helping his family grow food and find food in nature.

"During these hard times, I learned how to farm and survive. I also learned why it's important to care for nature and respect our traditions."

Education & Community Leadership

2000s - Liberia

When the war ended, James went back to Liberia to finish school. He studied how people live together at the University of Liberia. He became a leader by working with youth groups and his church, where he helped young people all over the country.

Later, he traveled to different countries in West Africa teaching young people how to make their communities better and solve problems peacefully.

Environmental Awakening

2010 - Guinea

An important change happened when James visited his old village in Guinea. The village was empty because the trees had been cut down, making the river too dirty to use. The people had to move away.

"This changed everything for me. I saw how cutting down too many trees can hurt whole communities."

Environmental Leadership

2013-Present - West Africa

James's work experience:

  • Studied at the Centre for Social Excellence (Earthworm Foundation)
  • Helped companies be greener at Golden Veroleum Liberia
  • Managed projects for Earthworm Foundation in Ghana
  • Now works helping businesses protect nature at Rainforest Builder Ghana
